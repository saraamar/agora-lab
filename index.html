Why AGORA works on every dimension

As a classical reference

The agora was where Athenian commerce, governance, and civic debate converged — exactly the intersection your lab studies: industrial systems, human decision-making, and community economics.

As an acronym

Applied — field-based, not theoretical. Global — research sites span Ohio, Morocco, UAE, West Africa. Operations Research & Analytics — your ISE disciplinary home, named explicitly.

In French and Arabic

Agora (أغورا) is used directly in French and Arabic academic writing. No translation needed. Labo AGORA reads naturally. The Moroccan site carries the same name without losing anything.

As a lab identity

The agora was not a building — it was a place that existed because people chose to gather there. AGORA Lab is not a room at BGSU. It is a network that meets at the intersection of industrial systems and the real world.

"We study what happens when intelligent systems enter workplaces and economies that most operations research has yet to fully examine — and we build the analytical tools to make those systems work better for the people inside them."

The animating question

When AI and automation enter an industrial system, who gains decision-making power — and who loses it?

This question runs through every project in AGORA Lab: the HMLV manufacturing work in Ohio, the cooperative resilience fieldwork in Morocco, the SME AI adoption study, the AfCFTA digital trade proposal, the Amazon seller decision-support experiment. They are all versions of the same inquiry.

What the lab stands for

Applied over abstract

Every AGORA project has a field component, an industry partner, or a real decision-maker as a stakeholder. Methods serve problems — not the other way around.

Global over provincial

AGORA treats Ohio and Morocco as co-equal research territories. The lab's intellectual geography is deliberately wider than its home institution.

Systems over silos

Industrial engineering, development economics, human factors, and logistics are not separate disciplines in this lab — they are lenses on the same set of problems.

People over throughput

Industry 5.0 is the intellectual frame: human-centricity and resilience over automation-first efficiency. Optimization that excludes is not optimization.

What AGORA does not do

AGORA Lab does not publish theoretical AI papers with no real-world grounding. It does not study well-resourced firms with clean data as its primary subject. It is not a consulting arm, a software lab, or a lab organized around a single funded project. It is a sustained inquiry into how industrial systems behave in the places and populations most operations research has overlooked.

Mood direction

Grounded, not generic

Real photographs from field sites. Moroccan markets, Toledo factory floors, cooperative meetings. Never stock imagery of data visualizations or glowing circuits.

Warm, not institutional

The terracotta palette signals that this lab cares about the material world — buildings, people, economies — not abstract systems. Engineering with texture.

Precise, not busy

Generous whitespace. Two typeface weights. The research speaks — the design does not compete with it or perform sophistication on its behalf.

Logo rationale

The ascending bars in the logo mark are deliberately ambiguous: a bar chart (operations analytics), a Greek column (the agora), and a population histogram (the people the lab studies). All three readings are simultaneously true. The dark ground anchors it. The terracotta-to-sand progression mirrors the palette and the gradient from well-resourced to constrained economies — the territory AGORA deliberately moves across.
