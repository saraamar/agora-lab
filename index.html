The animating question

When AI and automation enter an operation — a factory, a cooperative, a trading corridor — who gains decision-making power and who loses it?

This question runs through every project in AGORA Lab. The methods change — operations research, field experiments, multi-criteria decision analysis, management science. The question does not.

Operations & management first. AGORA is grounded in industrial and systems engineering, operations management, and management science. AI is a tool we study — not the discipline we practice.

Applied over abstract. Every AGORA project has a field component, an industry partner, or a real decision-maker as a stakeholder. Methods serve problems, not the other way around.

Advanced methods, broader contexts. AGORA examines how modern operations management and manufacturing practices — Industry 5.0, AI-assisted decision-making, optimization, lean systems — perform across a wide range of organizational scales and settings, from advanced manufacturers to SMEs worldwide. The comparison between what works in well-resourced environments and what transfers to constrained ones is where the most generalizable and impactful knowledge is produced.

Industry 5.0 as the frame. Human-centricity and resilience over automation-first efficiency. A system that optimizes throughput while excluding workers is not a well-designed system.

Why join AGORA

Three doors into the lab.

AGORA Lab operates as a network rather than a physical space. It offers three distinct pathways for engagement, tailored to students, industry partners, and international research collaborators, each defined by a unique form of participation and contribution.

01

Graduate students & researchers

AGORA is looking for students who want to do research that goes somewhere — into manufacturing plants, cooperative management offices, and trade ministries, not just journals. We offer field experience across multiple continents, an international network spanning MENA, sub-Saharan Africa, and East Asia, and direct industry exposure through active partnerships in the Great Lakes region. If you are drawn to industrial engineering, operations management, manufacturing systems, or development economics — and want your dissertation to matter outside academia — this is your lab.


Apply to join →
02

Industry & manufacturing partners

AGORA works with manufacturers, logistics operators, and operations managers who want rigorous analytical work — not generic consulting. We bring operations research methods, optimization modeling, field experiment design, and management science expertise. What we need from industry partners: data access, site access, and co-PI participation in funded research. Current partners include manufacturing firms and innovation consortia in Northwest Ohio. We are actively seeking new industry co-investigators for NSF GOALI and NOIC proposals in manufacturing systems, circular economy operations, and human-AI integration.


Explore partnership →
03

International academic collaborators

AGORA is actively building a network of researchers across the Global South, MENA, sub-Saharan Africa, and Southeast Asia who study operations management, manufacturing systems, industrial engineering, and development economics. We welcome partners in three forms: field access and co-authorship on proposals targeting marginalized or under-researched economies; co-supervision of doctoral students across institutions; and participation as named collaborators on international grant proposals including the Tohoku Forum 2028, Hinrich Foundation, and Schmidt Foundation submissions. We acknowledge the asymmetries of international academic collaboration honestly — AGORA is a genuine partner, not an extractive research relationship.

People

Contributors, not credentials.

At AGORA Lab, individuals are defined by their contributions to the core research questions rather than by formal titles. The lab is purposefully small and highly engaged, emphasizing hands-on, field-based work over distant observation.

SA

Sara Amar, PhD

Founder & Principal Investigator · AGORA Lab

Assistant Professor, Industrial & Logistics Engineering · Bowling Green State University · Program Coordinator, Systems Engineering

Sara's research sits at the intersection of operations management, manufacturing systems engineering, human-machine collaboration, and economic development. She examines how technology adoption — particularly AI and digital tools — reshapes operations, labor, and organizational outcomes in resource-constrained and SME-dominated economies across the Global South. Her work spans facility layout and optimization, artisanal cooperative resilience, human-centric AI adoption barriers in manufacturing, and logistics system design in low-data environments. She is committed to operations research and management science that is applied, global, and honest about the communities it serves.
